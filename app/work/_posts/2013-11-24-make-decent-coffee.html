This was a really fun but challenging project, Make Decent Coffee wanted an exhibition stand designing to promote their new e-commerce website. They had paid for a huge 10x6m spot at The London Coffee Festival - wow.

For those of you who have never heard about The London Coffee Festival, it is huge 4 day event in London. Over 20,000 coffee lovers go there every year, that is a lot of potential customers! It was crucial that the stand was engaging as possible, Make Decent Coffee wanted to be able to interact with the crowd and get them doing what their namesake says - Making Decent Coffee.

I was lead designer on this project and I brought together my concept of turning the industrial space into a homely, quirky living area which would have live demonstrations on tables which would be in the centre of the stand. This would create the engagement with the visitors.

I used illustrations which would be printed as vinyls, to add elements such as curtains, bookcases, shelves and lamps, then combined this with seating (there is never enough seating at these events) and screen-printed cushions. This helped to create a cool & trendy looking space which stood out from the crowd.

There was a lot of large scale printing from various printers involved with this project, so that meant a lot of planning and co-ordinating to make sure everything was delivered on time.
