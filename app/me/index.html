Biography

For as long as I can remember I've been designing - even before I knew what designing was. Something about the process of combining creativity and creating appealed to me. It has stuck ever since.

As a child growing up in York I dreamt of becoming a scientist. It wasn't until I chose to study Graphic Design over Design Technology that I realised design would be my occupation. Science would have to be a backup plan.

I studied Graphic Design further at college and university. I enjoyed broadening my skills and learning key design principles. During this time I gained a strong appreciation for typography and <something>.

After university I joined the coffee world working as an in-house designer for Coopers Coffee. Before this job I was happy drinking an instant coffee, nowadays my favourite brew is a nice single-origin brewed with a V60 pourover. Yes, unfortunately I have become a coffee snob.

Coopers really helped me develop professionally. The role really developed my skills in packaging design (and the rules that are followed) and real-world branding.

In 2011 I moved from Yorkshire to London to join United Coffee where I am currently working. This gave me the opportunity to work on larger projects with a talented team and to broaden my skills further into web and other digital design.

My role gives me an opportunity to work on lots of aspects of design. From event stands, to branding, and frequently packaging. <some more stuff>

I have an appreciation for subtlety in design which I like to apply to my work. Simple things, like matching colours, careful use of space can make the difference to a design.

While not working I have been renovating my flat with my partner. It is great to see my designs for the rooms come to life albeit a slow process. I also enjoy keeping fit, practicing the cosmopolitan sports of Yoga and Running. This is particularly helpful since my vice is a fondness for foods involving cheese.

On the internet I run a photo blog documenting the ever changing street art of Shoreditch,. I keep a Pinterest of design inspiration. I use my Flickr to document both my travels and experiments with analogue photography. Occasionally I post work in progress to my dribbble.

If you'd like to get in touch with me you can email me, tweet me, or find me on Linked In.
